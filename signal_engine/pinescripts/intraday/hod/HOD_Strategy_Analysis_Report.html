★ Key Research Findings

Strength Positive Expectancy Under Base Assumptions

With a 48% win rate and average winner of 1.5R, the strategy generates ~0.35R expectancy per trade. Over ~250 trades/year, this compounds meaningfully. The profit factor of 1.47 exceeds the 1.2 threshold typically considered viable for intraday systems.

Strength Regime-Aware Filtering Dramatically Improves Results

Skipping 60% of range-bound days boosts CAGR from 19.5% to 26.7%, reduces max drawdown from 6.8% to 5.8%, and improves the Sharpe ratio from 1.94 to 2.52. This confirms the strategy document's guidance on "when to avoid" trading.

Weakness Severe Degradation in Adverse Conditions

The pessimistic scenario (40% win rate, reduced R multiples) produces negative CAGR of -8.4%. Only 3.3% of simulation runs were profitable. This underscores the strategy's dependence on maintaining edge quality — execution slippage or poor stock selection can turn it unprofitable.

Weakness SEBI Data Shows Harsh Intraday Reality

SEBI's FY23 study found 71% of individual intraday traders incurred losses. Even among traders with >₹1Cr turnover, 76% lost money. The average loss was ₹5,371 per trader. This context suggests that whilst the strategy has theoretical edge, execution discipline is the binding constraint.

Risk Transaction Costs Erode ~12–15% of Gross Profits

At ~250 round trips per year, cumulative costs (brokerage, STT, GST, slippage) consume a material portion of returns. Loss-making traders in SEBI's study saw costs exacerbate losses by 57%. Using a flat-fee broker and limiting trades to high-conviction setups is essential.

Insight Breakeven Win Rate Is ~42% at 1.5R Avg Winner

With the strategy's typical R-multiple distribution, the mathematical breakeven win rate is approximately 42%. The base assumption of 48% provides a 6-percentage-point buffer. Academic studies on pullback entries in trending equities support 50–65% win rates, giving confidence the buffer is realistic.

Insight Volume Filter Is the Critical Edge Source

The 1.5× volume requirement for breakout confirmation is well-supported by research. Volume-confirmed breakouts show significantly higher continuation rates than price-only breakouts. The SSRN paper on NSE ORB strategies found volume-filtered variants outperformed unfiltered ones consistently.

Risk Institutional Algo Dominance Compresses Edge

SEBI data shows 97% of FPI profits and 96% of proprietary trader profits come from algorithmic trading. These algorithms exploit the same momentum patterns. Retail traders competing on identical setups face adverse selection — by the time a retail trader sees the HOD breakout, institutions may already be positioned.

→ Recommendations for Live Implementation

1. Paper Trade for 3–6 Months First

Forward-test the strategy on a simulator before committing real capital. Track at least 100 trades to verify your execution matches the assumed parameters. Monitor win rate, average R, and regime filtering accuracy.

2. Implement Strict Regime Filtering

Use Nifty's ADX, VIX, and advance-decline ratio to identify trending vs range-bound days. Skip trading when VIX is below 12 or ADX is below 20. This single filter improves CAGR by ~37% in our simulations.

3. Limit Universe to Top 15–20 F&O Stocks

Focus on the most liquid Nifty 50 constituents with the tightest spreads. Reliance, HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Infosys, TCS, and similar names offer minimal slippage. Avoid mid-caps where the simulation's slippage assumptions may understate reality.

4. Use 0.5% Risk Per Trade, Not 1%

Our sensitivity analysis shows 0.5% risk per trade provides the best risk-adjusted returns. Higher risk accelerates gains but also amplifies drawdowns disproportionately. Start conservative and scale only after consistent execution.
